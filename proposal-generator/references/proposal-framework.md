# Proposal Framework by Deal Type

## Format Selection Guide

| Deal Size | Employee Count | Format | Pages | Turnaround |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SMB transactional | <50 | One-pager | 1 | Same day |
| Mid-market standard | 50–500 | Standard proposal | 3–5 | 24–48 hrs |
| Enterprise / committee | 500+ | Full business case | 5–8 | 2–3 days |

---

## One-Pager Structure (SMB)

**Goal:** Decision in one read. Everything on one page.

```
[Header: Company name + our logo]

THE CHALLENGE
2-3 bullet points mirroring their pain in their words

OUR SOLUTION
What we're proposing, in plain language

WHAT YOU GET
3 concrete outcomes with metrics where possible

INVESTMENT
Price — clear, no surprises

NEXT STEP
One action with a date
```

**Tone:** Direct, warm, no jargon.
**Length:** Strictly 1 page.

## Standard Proposal Structure (Mid-Market)

### Section 1: Executive Summary (½ page)
- 3 sentences: situation, consequence of inaction, proposed outcome
- Make it stand alone — a stakeholder who only reads this should get the full picture
- End with a clear ask: "We recommend moving forward by [date] to [achieve outcome]"

### Section 2: Your Current Challenge (½–1 page)
- Mirror back their pain points using their exact language from discovery
- Quantify impact: "This means your team spends X hours per week on Y" or "This is costing approximately $Z per quarter"
- Show you understand their world better than they expected
- Never use product language in this section — it's about them, not you

### Section 3: Our Recommended Approach (1 page)
- What you're proposing (solution overview, not feature list)
- Why this approach for their specific situation
- What's in scope (and what's not)
- Phased approach if complexity warrants it
- Timeline to go-live and time to first value

### Section 4: Expected Outcomes & ROI (½–1 page)
- Lead with the business outcome, not the feature
- Quantified projection tied to their specific metrics:
  - Time saved: "X hours/rep/week × Y reps × $Z loaded cost = $A/year"
  - Revenue impact: "X% improvement in [metric] × current $Y = $Z opportunity"
  - Cost reduction: "Eliminate [current spend] on [tool/process] = $X savings"
- Conservative + realistic scenario if appropriate
- Customer reference: "[Similar company] achieved [result] in [timeframe]"

### Section 5: Why Us — 3 Differentiators (½ page)
Tie each differentiator to a criterion they expressed:
- "You said [criterion]. Here's how we address that: [specific differentiator]."
- Include one customer proof point per differentiator
- Address the biggest risk or concern proactively

### Section 6: Investment (½ page)
- Present pricing clearly — never bury it
- What's included (support tier, onboarding, seats, etc.)
- Contract term and renewal terms
- Optional: two tiers (recommended vs. starter) to anchor the right option
- Payment terms if relevant

### Section 7: Next Steps (¼ page)
- Step 1: [Action] — by [Date] — Owner: [Prospect name]
- Step 2: [Action] — by [Date] — Owner: [Our name]
- Step 3: Go-live — [Target Date]
- "To get started, simply [one clear action]"

## Full Business Case Structure (Enterprise)

Add these sections to the Standard Proposal:

### Risk & Mitigation
- Top 3 risks the prospect might be worried about
- Specific mitigation for each
- Contractual protections (SLA, exit clauses, data portability)

### Implementation Plan
- Week-by-week timeline
- Resource requirements from their side (be specific and honest)
- Milestones and success criteria
- Change management considerations

### Stakeholder Value Map
| Stakeholder | Their Priority | How We Address It |
|---|---|---|
| CEO/CFO | ROI, risk | ROI model, SLA |
| IT/Security | Compliance, integration | SOC2, API docs |
| End users | Ease of use, time savings | UX, onboarding |
| Champion | Career win, internal credibility | Quick wins, exec support |

### Appendix
- Technical specs (for IT reviewers)
- Security and compliance overview
- Customer references (3 similar companies)
- Contract summary

## Writing Rules

1. **Lead with them, not you.** First 2 pages should be about their problem, not your product.
2. **Use their words.** If they said "our pipeline is a mess" in discovery, say "pipeline visibility" — not "sales analytics."
3. **Make the ROI math transparent.** Show the formula, not just the conclusion.
4. **One CTA.** Don't give them three ways to move forward. Give them one.
5. **Design matters.** A clean, well-formatted proposal signals professionalism. Use the template.

## Common Proposal Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Generic executive summary | Write it last, after you know all the details |
| Feature list instead of outcomes | For every feature, ask "so what does this mean for them?" |
| ROI numbers without sources | Always show your math or cite the customer |
| No next steps | End every proposal with a specific ask + date |
| Too long | Cut anything that doesn't help them decide |
