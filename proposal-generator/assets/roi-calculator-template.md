# ROI Calculator Template

Use this to build a credible, prospect-specific ROI model for proposals.

---

| Current tool cost (if replacing) | | Prospect confirmed |
| Current conversion rate (if improving) | | CRM data or estimate |
| Average deal size | | Prospect confirmed |
| Deals per rep per quarter | | Prospect confirmed |

---

## Model 1: Time Savings ROI

```
Hours saved per rep per week:        [X hrs]
× Reps:                              [Y reps]
= Total hours saved per week:        [X×Y hrs]
× 50 working weeks:                  [X×Y×50 hrs/year]
× Hourly cost (loaded salary/2000):  $[Z/hr]
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Annual time value recovered:         $[Result]
```

**Conservative scenario (50% of above):** $[Result × 0.5]
**Realistic scenario (75% of above):** $[Result × 0.75]

---

## Model 2: Revenue Impact ROI

```
Current deals closed per rep per quarter:    [A]
Expected improvement in conversion rate:     [B%]  ← use customer benchmarks
Additional deals per rep per quarter:        [A × B%]
× Average deal size:                         $[C]
× Number of reps:                            [Y]
× 4 quarters:                               ×4
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Annual revenue uplift:                       $[Result]
```

**Use this when:** Your product improves win rate, shortens sales cycle, or improves pipeline coverage.

---

## Model 3: Cost Reduction ROI

```
Current tool/process cost:           $[A/year]
Tools being replaced:                [Tool 1: $X, Tool 2: $Y]
New investment:                      $[B/year]
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Net savings:                         $[A - B]
```

## Payback Period Formula

```
Payback period (months) = (Annual cost / 12) ÷ (Monthly value)
```

---

## Formatting for the Proposal

Present the model in plain language first, then show the table:

> "Based on your team of [Y] reps spending approximately [X hours] per week on [task],
> we estimate [product] would recover [Z hours/week] of selling time.
> At a fully-loaded rep cost of $[C]/year, that represents approximately $[value] in
> recovered productivity annually — a [X]x return on your investment within [Y] months."

Always note: "These projections are based on results seen with similar customers. Your results may vary based on adoption rate and use case."
