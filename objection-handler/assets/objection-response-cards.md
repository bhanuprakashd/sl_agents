# Objection Response Quick-Reference Cards

Use these as flash cards during live calls. Each card: objection → immediate response → follow-up question.

---

## PRICE

**"Too expensive"**
> "That's fair — let's make sure the value is clear before we talk price. What would solving [pain] be worth to your team in a year?"

**"No budget"**
> "Understood. Is this a timing issue, or is there truly no appetite for this investment right now?"

**"Cheaper alternatives exist"**
> "There are. What matters most to you beyond price? I want to make sure we're comparing the right things."

---

## TIMING

**"Come back next quarter"**
> "Happy to. Can I ask — what changes next quarter that makes this a better time?"

**"Too busy right now"**
> "I get it. Is the busyness related to [the problem we solve], or something else entirely?"

**"Not a priority"**
> "Makes sense. What IS the priority right now? I want to understand where this fits relative to that."

---

## COMPETITOR

**"We use [Competitor]"**
> "How's that going? What do you love about it, and what do you work around?"

**"Evaluating [Competitor]"**
> "Good to know. What are the top criteria you're using to compare?"

**"[Competitor] is cheaper"**
> "It is. What would a cheaper option need to do for it to be the right call for you?"

---

## AUTHORITY

**"Need to check with boss"**
> "Of course. What's your read — how do you think they'll see it?"

**"Not my decision"**
> "Totally understand. Who owns this? And what's your role in the process?"

---

## NEED

**"We don't have that problem"**
> "Good to know — tell me what your current process looks like. I don't want to assume."

**"We're fine with what we have"**
> "I hear that. Is it working well, or working well enough that fixing it isn't urgent yet?"

---

## TRUST

**"Never heard of you"**
> "Fair. Would it help to talk to a customer similar to you? Happy to set up a reference call."

**"You're too small"**
> "What's the specific concern — support, security, contract terms? I want to address the right thing."

---

## COMPLEXITY

**"Seems hard to implement"**
> "What part worries you most — the technical side, or getting internal buy-in?"

**"No bandwidth"**
> "I hear you. What would 'low enough effort' look like for you? Phased rollout, lighter onboarding?"

---

## UNIVERSAL STALL BREAKERS

When a call stalls and you're not sure what the real objection is:

> "It seems like something's giving you pause that we haven't fully addressed. What is it?"

> "What would need to be true for this to be a clear yes?"

> "If timeline and budget weren't factors, would this be something you'd want to move forward with?"

> "What's your gut telling you?"

---

## The Break-Up Line

When a deal has stalled for too long:

> "I want to respect your time. Is this something you see moving forward in the next [30/60/90 days], or should I close this out for now and we reconnect when the timing is better?"
