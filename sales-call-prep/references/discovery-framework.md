# Discovery Framework & Question Bank

## MEDDIC Qualification Framework

Use MEDDIC to qualify deals and structure discovery. Each letter = a dimension to uncover.

| Letter | Stands For | What to Uncover |
|---|---|---|
| **M** | Metrics | Quantified impact — what does success look like in numbers? |
| **E** | Economic Buyer | Who signs the check? Have we talked to them? |
| **D** | Decision Criteria | What will they use to choose a vendor? |
| **D** | Decision Process | What are the steps from here to signed? |
| **I** | Identify Pain | What's the specific, felt pain driving this evaluation? |
| **C** | Champion | Who wants this internally and will fight for it? |

---

## Question Bank by Category

### Pain Discovery
*Goal: Surface specific, felt pain with business impact*

- "What's the biggest challenge your team is running into right now with [area]?"
- "What does that problem cost you — in time, money, or missed opportunity?"
- "How long has this been an issue? What's triggered the evaluation now?"
- "If you solved this tomorrow, what would change?"
- "What have you tried already? Why didn't it work?"
- "On a scale of 1–10, how painful is this? What would make it a 10?"

### Metrics & Impact
*Goal: Quantify the pain and the value of solving it*

- "What does good look like? How would you measure success?"
- "What's the cost of not solving this — this quarter, this year?"
- "If you improved [metric] by X%, what does that mean for the business?"
- "What KPIs does your team own? How is [problem] affecting them?"

### Economic Buyer & Authority
*Goal: Map the buying process and find the real decision maker*

- "Who else is involved in evaluating this?"
- "Who ultimately approves a purchase like this?"
- "Have you done a similar purchase before? How did that process work?"
- "What does your procurement process look like?"
- "Is there a budget already allocated, or would this need to be approved?"

### Decision Criteria & Process
*Goal: Understand how they'll decide and on what timeline*

- "What would the ideal solution need to do for this to be a clear yes?"
- "Are you evaluating other options? What are you comparing?"
- "What would make you choose us over the alternative?"
- "What could disqualify a vendor at this stage?"
- "If everything checked out, what does the path to a decision look like?"
- "What's your timeline? Is there a hard deadline driving this?"

### Champion Identification
*Goal: Find and develop your internal advocate*

- "Who on your team would benefit most from solving this?"
- "Who would you say is most invested in getting this right?"
- "If I sent you a one-pager, who else should get a copy?"
- "What does success on this project mean for you personally?"

### Urgency & Timing
*Goal: Create or uncover urgency*

- "What happens if this isn't solved by [timeframe]?"
- "Is there a specific event or deadline this needs to be live before?"
- "What's your priority level on this vs. other initiatives right now?"
- "What would cause this to get deprioritized?"

## SPICED Framework (Alternative to MEDDIC)

Better for product-led or shorter sales cycles:

| Letter | Stands For | Question Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Situation | Current state — what does their world look like now? |
| **P** | Pain | What's not working? What's the cost? |
| **I** | Impact | What happens if they don't fix it? |
| **C** | Critical Event | Is there a deadline or trigger driving urgency? |
| **E** | Decision | Who decides, how, and by when? |

---

## Questions to Avoid

- "Do you have a budget?" (too direct too early — use "How does investment in this area typically get approved?")
- "Are you the decision maker?" (condescending — use "Who else is involved in decisions like this?")
- "Why haven't you fixed this already?" (accusatory — use "What's gotten in the way of solving this until now?")
- Yes/no questions that kill conversation flow

## By Call Type

### First Discovery Call — Priority Questions
1. What triggered the evaluation now?
2. What does the problem cost (time/money)?
3. Who else is involved in this?
4. What does success look like?
5. What's the timeline?

### Second Call / Deep Dive
1. What did you share internally after our last call?
2. What questions came up from the team?
3. Have you evaluated [metric impact] further?
4. What are the must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?

### Pre-Close Call
1. What would need to be true for you to move forward?
2. Is there anything that could block this internally?
3. What does the contract/procurement process look like?
4. If we can meet your criteria, are you ready to move forward by [date]?
