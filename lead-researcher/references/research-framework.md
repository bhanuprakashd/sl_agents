# Research Framework by Segment

Tailored research angles based on company type, size, and industry.

## By Company Stage

### Seed / Early Stage (<50 employees)
**What to look for:**
- Founders' background — prior companies, domain expertise
- What problem they're solving and for whom
- Investor names (signal credibility and network)
- Speed of hiring (growth signal)

**Pain points to probe:**
- Moving fast, limited ops bandwidth
- Wearing many hats, need to automate
- Proving product-market fit under pressure

**Best outreach angle:** Founder-to-founder empathy, speed-to-value, low setup overhead

---

### Growth Stage (50–200 employees, Series A–B)
**What to look for:**
- Recent hires in RevOps, Sales Ops, Marketing Ops
- Stack consolidation or expansion signals
- New geographic or product expansion
- Board and investor pressures on metrics

**Pain points to probe:**
- Scaling processes built for a 10-person team
- Inconsistent handoffs between teams
- Reporting is manual and error-prone

**Best outreach angle:** "You've outgrown your current tools" — process maturity, scale

---

### Scale-Up (200–1000 employees, Series C+)
**What to look for:**
- Department-level buyers (VP/Director vs. C-suite)
- Existing vendor relationships (displacement opportunity?)
- M&A activity (integration complexity = pain)
- IPO prep signals

**Pain points to probe:**
- Cross-functional alignment and visibility
- Compliance and security requirements emerging
- Multiple tools doing overlapping jobs

**Best outreach angle:** ROI, integration story, enterprise readiness

---

## By Industry

### SaaS Companies
- Check G2 / Capterra reviews for their product — what customers love/hate informs their internal priorities
- Job postings for "RevOps" or "Sales Ops" signal investment in sales infrastructure
- Look at their pricing page: usage-based = they care about activation and expansion

### Fintech
- Regulatory environment shapes their timeline and risk tolerance
- Series B+ fintechs often rebuilding their ops stack after hypergrowth
- Check for banking partnerships or licensing — signals maturity stage

### E-commerce / D2C
- Revenue seasonality matters — avoid outreach near peak seasons (Q4)
- Tech stack heavily tied to Shopify ecosystem or custom
- Funding rounds often tied to inventory or growth campaigns

### Professional Services / Agencies
- Often under-resourced for ops — one person wears many hats
- Client deliverable quality is their brand — anything that helps output quality resonates
- Referral-driven growth = relationships matter, warm intros are gold

---

## Research Sources (Priority Order)

1. **Company website** — About, Blog, Careers, Press
2. **LinkedIn** — Company page, employees, job postings, leadership posts
3. **Crunchbase / PitchBook** — Funding, investors, headcount growth
4. **G2 / Capterra** — Product reviews (for SaaS prospects, reveals their customer pain)
5. **News search** — Google News, TechCrunch, industry publications
6. **Twitter/X** — Executive thought leadership, product announcements
7. **Job postings** — Best proxy for current priorities and tech stack

## Research Time Budget

| Prospect Priority | Time Budget | Depth |
|---|---|---|
| Hot / referred | 30–45 min | Full profile |
| Warm / ICP fit | 15–20 min | Core sections only |
| Cold / low signal | 5–10 min | Quick scan + 1 hook |
