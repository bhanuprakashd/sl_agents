# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Criteria

Use this to score prospects and flag poor-fit accounts before investing research time.

## Firmographic Fit

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Poor Fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employee count | 50–500 | 20–1000 | <20 or >5000 |
| Revenue | $5M–$200M | $1M–$500M | <$1M |
| Funding stage | Series A–C | Seed, Series D | Pre-seed, public enterprise |
| Geography | NA, UK, EU | ANZ, LATAM | APAC (check with AE) |

## Industry Fit

**Strong fit:**
- SaaS / Software
- Fintech
- E-commerce / D2C
- Professional Services (consulting, agencies)
- Healthcare tech

**Moderate fit:**
- Manufacturing (with digital ops)
- Real estate tech
- EdTech

**Poor fit:**
- Government / public sector
- Non-profit
- Heavy industry / construction

## Technographic Signals

**Positive signals (they likely have budget and are modern-stack):**
- Uses Salesforce, HubSpot, or modern CRM
- Slack or Teams for internal comms
- Cloud-first infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Active hiring for data/analytics roles

**Negative signals:**
- No CRM or uses spreadsheets only
- On-premise only infrastructure
- No engineering team

## Behavioral Signals

**Hot signals:**
- Recent funding round (last 6 months)
- Hiring aggressively in our product's domain
- Competitor just raised or got acquired
- Leadership change (new VP/CTO)
- Expanding to new market or product line

**Warm signals:**
- Job postings mention pain we solve
- Published content about a challenge we address
- Attended industry event we sponsor

## Fit Scoring

Score the prospect 1–5 on each category:
- **4–5**: Strong ICP, prioritize
- **3**: Borderline, proceed with lower time investment
- **1–2**: Flag for AE review before pursuing

## Notes for Researcher

- When in doubt, flag it — let the AE decide
- Always note the strongest fit signals in the profile summary
- A single "hot signal" can elevate a borderline prospect
